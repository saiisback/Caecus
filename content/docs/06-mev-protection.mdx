**Layer:** Infrastructure · **Primitive:** Encrypted Mempool

MEV protection is a **structural property** of Caecus, not a bolt-on. Because all transactions are encrypted before entering the mempool, front-running and sandwich attacks are cryptographically impossible.

## What this means

- Transaction data is encrypted at submission — sequencers see only ciphertexts.
- No bot can read a pending swap, perp order, or liquidation before it settles.
- Transaction ordering cannot be exploited because the intent is invisible.
- Fair ordering by default — **not by policy, but by math**.

## Why it matters

MEV extraction costs DeFi users billions per year. Caecus eliminates the attack surface entirely — not by adding a protected relay, but by making the underlying data unreadable.

Bots can't front-run what they can't see.
